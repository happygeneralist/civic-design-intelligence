# Repository Governance and Legal Basis
*Why and how this research repository complies with UK GDPR*

---

## 1. Purpose
This repository enables evidence-based service design by allowing insights and anonymised quotes to be stored and reused across projects. It helps prevent duplicated research and supports transparency in public decision-making.

---

## 2. Lawful Basis
Processing is carried out under **Article 6(1)(e) – Public Task** of the UK GDPR: performing a task in the public interest or in the exercise of official authority.  

Where special category data (e.g., SEND, health, or ethnicity) is processed, the council relies on **Article 9(2)(j)** – processing necessary for research purposes with appropriate safeguards.

Consent may still be collected from participants for ethical reasons, but it is not the sole legal basis for processing.

---

## 3. Scope of Data Stored
- **Included:** anonymised quotes, coded insights, study summaries, themes, and tags.  
- **Excluded:** raw transcripts, recordings, identifiable participant data.

This ensures only minimal personal data is held.

---

## 4. Retention and Storage
- **Raw data** (audio, transcripts) – not stored, retained for study consent, then deleted or archived securely.  
- **Anonymised insights/quotes** – can be stored indefinitely for research and archiving, provided they remain non-identifiable.  
- Repository access and storage are Microsoft-compliant (e.g., GitHub Enterprise, Azure, or SharePoint).

---

## 5. Safeguards
- Role-based access control (only approved researchers).  
- Encryption at rest and in transit.  
- Audit trail via version control.  
- Annual review of anonymisation, retention, and DPIA.

---

## 6. Rights and Transparency
Participants have the right to:  
- Access their data (if still identifiable).  
- Withdraw consent (if consent-based processing applies).  
- Request correction or deletion of identifiable data.  

A summary of repository governance should be published internally or publicly as a transparency statement.

---

## 7. Governance Ownership
- **Data Controller:** The council.  
- **Data Protection Officer:** Responsible for oversight and responding to subject access requests.  
- **Research Operations Lead:** Maintains documentation, templates, and training.  
- **Contributors:** Ensure anonymisation and correct tagging of insights.

---

## 8. Review and Audit
- DPIA reviewed annually or when major changes occur.  
- Random sample audits of repository content every 6–12 months.  
- Findings logged and reviewed by the DPO and ResearchOps lead.
