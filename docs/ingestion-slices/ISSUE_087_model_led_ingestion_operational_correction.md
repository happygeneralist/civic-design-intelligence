## Purpose

Clarify the scope of the model-led ingestion correction added in PR #88.

That correction was right to challenge an overly narrow evidence-first follow-up. However, its two-lane framing was too binary and too centred on coherent journey or service models.

The general repository principle is broader:

```text
Knowledge may enter in different forms and at different levels of maturity.
Capture it at the smallest useful level that preserves meaning and relationships.
Make its basis, assumptions and review state visible.
Strengthen, challenge or supersede it over time.
```

A whole assumptive journey model is one valid worked case. It is not the definition of provisional or non-linear ingestion.

## What remains correct from PR #88

The Essex review should not have treated direct lived-experience research as a prerequisite for the next repository step.

The existing public-information journey analysis can enter as a provisional view because it already contains useful expert and analytical work.

It should not be discarded or rebuilt from scratch simply because:

- its evidence chain is incomplete;
- some phases or relationships are assumptive;
- it mixes public comments, practitioner knowledge, system analysis and analyst synthesis;
- later research may change its structure.

Direct lived-experience research remains valuable as a strengthening, challenge and reframing source rather than an entry gate.

## What this clarification changes

The repository should not be understood as having only two ingestion lanes:

```text
Evidence-led
versus
Model-led
```

Instead, ingestion treatment should respond to:

- the **form** of the useful input;
- the **basis** of its important claims;
- its current **maturity**;
- the level at which assumptions or uncertainty exist;
- the smallest capture unit that preserves meaning.

Possible first treatments include:

- bounded source extraction;
- direct capture of an assumptive user need or pain point;
- provisional treatment of a relationship or causal hypothesis;
- preservation and selective structuring of a research summary;
- preservation of a generated journey or service view;
- whole-model capture where overall structure carries essential meaning;
- backward evidence-linking for legacy knowledge.

Whole-model preservation is conditional. It should be used where atomisation would destroy useful relationships or system-level meaning.

## Assumptive knowledge at different levels

An assumption may apply to:

- an entire object;
- one attribute, such as severity or actor scope;
- a relationship between objects;
- a causal explanation;
- applicability or prevalence;
- a journey phase or pattern;
- a whole generated view or model.

For example:

```text
Pain-point existence: locally evidenced
Severity: assumptive
National applicability: assumptive
Causal explanation: analyst hypothesis
Relationship to a user need: needs review
```

The repository should not imply that one object-level status describes every claim within an object equally well.

This does not require immediate schema expansion. Review notes and rationale should be used first.

## Worked journey-view treatment

Issue #89 remains useful as one bounded test:

> Test ingestion of an assumptive journey analysis as a provisional view.

For that specific input, preserve the original artefact because its whole structure is part of the value.

The test should:

1. preserve the original journey analysis intact and findable;
2. record its purpose, scope and intended use;
3. distinguish public evidence, practitioner knowledge, current-system analysis and analyst synthesis;
4. identify assumed phases, attributes and relationships;
5. distinguish service-defined sequencing from recurring lived-experience patterns where possible;
6. compare the view with existing evidence, needs, civic needs, pain points, behaviours, insights and themes;
7. connect or structure only the components that improve repository usefulness;
8. leave some material at view level where atomisation would reduce meaning;
9. expose contradictions, evidence gaps and high-value research questions;
10. keep all resulting interpretations at conservative maturity states.

The retrospective should state clearly what this journey-view test does and does not generalise to other input forms.

## Individual assumptive objects

An individual assumptive user need, civic need, pain point, behaviour or insight may be captured directly as a conservative candidate object when that is the smallest useful level that preserves meaning.

It does not need:

- a whole-model record;
- a journey framework;
- complete source-to-evidence traceability before entry;
- artificial expansion into a larger synthesis.

It does need:

- preservation of its original meaning and origin;
- appropriate writing and object-quality checks;
- nearby-object reconciliation;
- visible provenance and evidence gaps;
- conservative maturity and review metadata;
- explicit notes where only some attributes or relationships are assumptive.

## Relationship and explanation hypotheses

A useful claim may be about a relationship rather than a new object.

Examples include:

- unclear responsibility may lead to repeated chasing;
- communication gaps may compound waiting-related harm;
- a funding mechanism may shift navigation burden to families.

These connections should be recorded as provisional analysis or review notes unless an established relationship structure is clearly appropriate.

Do not make analyst inference look like observed or tested causation.

## Capture and use

Capture and promotion are different decisions.

Candidate or assumptive knowledge may be useful for:

- revealing the shape of the problem space;
- identifying gaps and contradictions;
- planning research;
- generating questions;
- creating provisional design views.

That does not automatically make it safe for:

- stable benchmarking;
- national claims;
- policy conclusions;
- consequential operational decisions;
- validation or substantiation.

The threshold should rise with the consequence of use.

## Operational responsibility for AI-assisted ingestion

Repository-aware agents must inspect authoritative ingestion guidance, relevant issues and nearby objects before selecting a treatment.

An agent must not:

- infer that the most recently tested route is the only route;
- ask the repository owner to restate documented principles;
- discard useful assumptive knowledge because traceability is incomplete;
- force every input through source-first extraction;
- force every provisional input through whole-model preservation;
- atomise coherent work before preserving its useful structure;
- treat coherence as validation;
- silently narrow the operating model.

When current work conflicts with authoritative guidance, the agent should:

1. identify the conflict;
2. inspect the repository authority chain;
3. preserve existing meaning and decisions;
4. propose the smallest correction;
5. record it in a reviewable issue or PR;
6. avoid transferring continuity burden back to the user.

## Finalising actions require explicit instruction

Creating or updating an issue, branch or pull request does not authorise merging, closing, deleting or otherwise finalising it.

Agents should stop at a reviewable PR unless the user explicitly instructs the finalising action.
