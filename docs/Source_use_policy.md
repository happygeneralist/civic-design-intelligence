# Source use policy

This repository is public-source-first.

User needs and civic needs are treated as reusable civic knowledge objects. They may exist as candidate public assets without being owned by a source organisation. Evidence links, source records and research-study records are separate provenance claims and must be public, openly licensed or explicitly authorised.

## Evidence provenance

Evidence provenance is managed separately from the existence of a user need, civic need, behaviour, pain point, insight or theme.

Generic candidate objects may exist without evidence, but they must not be treated as evidence-linked until supported by public or explicitly authorised evidence.

Local or source-specific evidence can support a general or umbrella object where the underlying need, pain, behaviour or civic obligation is not place-specific. The evidence link should make the source scope clear. A single local source may support the existence or local manifestation of an issue, but must not be used on its own to claim national prevalence, severity or consistency.

## Sources that may be used

Use sources as evidence only when they are:

- public
- openly licensed
- explicitly authorised for this repository
- primary research where consent, permission, ownership and publication constraints are clear

Suitable public-source rebuild inputs may include public Ofsted/CQC area SEND inspection reports, public Local Government and Social Care Ombudsman decisions, public SEND statutory guidance, public Local Offer pages, public council scrutiny or cabinet papers, public consultation outputs, public charity reports, public parliamentary reports and explicitly published reusable user research.

## Sources that must not be ingested without documented permission

Private employer, client, council, partner or unpublished research material must not be ingested unless permission is explicit and documented.

Primary research has a place in the repository only where consent, permission, ownership and publication constraints are clear.

## Candidate objects

Candidate user needs, civic needs, behaviours, pain points, insights and themes can be useful civic knowledge objects before they are evidence-linked.

When evidence links are removed, retained generic objects should be marked conservatively so they are not mistaken for supported findings. They should remain candidates or assumptions until public or explicitly authorised evidence supports them.
