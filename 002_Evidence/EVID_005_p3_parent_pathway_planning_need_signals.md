# P3 parent pathway planning need signals

## Evidence

This evidence note captures an anonymised summary of need signals from a parent/carer participant extract supplied for the initial pathway-planning guidance level test.

The supplied extract describes these evidence points:

- Support should start "right at the start", including from Year 8 or Year 9, because decisions otherwise arrive "all at once".
- The participant preferred the personalised helper because it would be "based around my child" and said generic step-by-step guidance would not be enough on its own.
- The participant said nobody had explained what would happen if her son failed GCSEs, which courses or routes would still be available, or how post-16 participation would work in practice.
- The participant described a careers conversation and report that did not reflect reality, and said her son can sound convincing while giving inaccurate answers, especially with unfamiliar adults.
- The participant said the conversation would be better with "someone that knows him", not necessarily a SENCO, but someone who understands him.
- The participant described relying on Facebook groups, teacher friends and ad hoc research, but said no one could give the "full picture".
- The participant stressed that college or training routes cannot be considered in isolation from transport, bus use, burnout, attendance patterns and what the young person can realistically manage.
- The participant called for a "reflection" stage to come back, check how things are going, and tweak support or choices if the original path is not right.
- The participant described having to research things herself, not knowing what she was entitled to ask for, and feeling left to work it out alone.
- The participant said she wants the team to "see him as opposed to a child that's on the spectrum" and not assume all children fit "one box".
