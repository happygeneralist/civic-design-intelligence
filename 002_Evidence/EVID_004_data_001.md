>level 1 courses only have 5% outcome rate

### Context
Level 1 qualifications are typically the first step on the vocational learning ladder. These are intended to provide a pathway for learners who may not yet be ready for GCSE-level study (Level 2) or for direct entry into employment. They are disproportionately accessed by:

- Learners with SEND
- Young people from disadvantaged backgrounds
- Those who have disengaged from mainstream education or have lower prior attainment

However, national data shows that **only around 5% of learners who start a Level 1 course achieve a positive outcome**, such as:

- Progressing to a higher qualification (Level 2 or above)
- Moving into paid employment
- Successfully completing the course with a recognised credential

In other words, **95% of students do not reach a sustained next step**, suggesting that the pathway either stalls or breaks down after entry.
