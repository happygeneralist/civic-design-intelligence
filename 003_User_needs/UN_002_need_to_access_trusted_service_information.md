# Access up-to-date, accurate and trusted information about available services, so I can confidently guide families to the right support

## Summary

Professionals responsible for supporting young people and families during pathway planning report that they often lack access to reliable and current information. Service directories are fragmented, outdated, or poorly maintained. As a result, professionals may unintentionally misdirect families or offer guidance they cannot verify.

## Value and impact

- **Perceived value:** Builds confidence and trust between professionals and families.
- **Civic value:** Improves equity and effectiveness in service access.
- **Measurement:** Accuracy and currency of signposting resources; reduction in misreferrals; increased professional confidence in guidance.
- **Decision rationale:** This need was prioritised due to consistent references across user groups and the direct link to breakdowns in pathway planning and trust.

## Public-source reset note

Retained as a generic candidate civic knowledge object. Source-specific evidence links were removed during the public-source reset. This object should not be treated as evidence-linked until supported by public or explicitly authorised sources.

## Expected outcomes

- Professionals become effective and trusted navigators within the system.
- Families receive accurate support, first time.
- Reduced administrative burden and frustration across the system.
