# Stay connected to my pathway even when my mental health or motivation dips, so I don’t lose progress or feel overwhelmed starting again

### Summary
Young people with SEND experience fluctuating motivation and mental health that disrupt ongoing pathway planning. When they disengage, the absence of continuity mechanisms (e.g., saved progress, supportive check-ins) leads to exhaustion and loss of confidence. They express a desire for ways to stay anchored to their plan even when active engagement isn’t possible.

### Value and impact
- **Perceived value:** Reduces guilt, overwhelm, and the emotional cost of restarting.  
- **Civic value:** Supports sustained engagement and improves progression outcomes.  
- **Measurement:** Frequency of re-engagement; drop-out recovery rates; user confidence in “getting back on track.”  
- **Decision rationale:** Anchoring mechanisms can prevent disengagement from becoming permanent exclusion.

### Evidence of success
- Young people report feeling “still on track” even during setbacks.
- Parents note reduced burnout and rework.
- Improved continuity across education, health, and work planning stages.

**Expected outcomes**
- Reduced drop-off due to mental health dips.
- Improved self-efficacy and confidence to re-engage.
- Greater emotional resilience in pathway planning.
