# Begin planning before key education choices become fixed

## Working formulation

A parent/carer of a young person with SEND needs to begin planning before key education choices become fixed, so they can make realistic decisions with enough time to act.

## Wording rationale

The wording uses `begin planning` rather than `start pathway planning early` to keep the need focused on the parent/carer's action and the timing constraint, without implying a specific service mechanism.

The phrase `before key education choices become fixed` preserves the evidence meaning that decisions can arrive too late or all at once, especially around GCSE and pathway decisions.

## Interpretation

The parent/carer evidence suggests that pathway planning can become high-stakes when support arrives after key decisions have already narrowed. The need is not only for information, but for enough time to interpret options, seek support and act before choices become fixed.
