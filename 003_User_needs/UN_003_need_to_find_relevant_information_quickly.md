# Quickly find information that is relevant to my situation, so I don't waste time navigating things that don't apply to me

## Summary

Parents report that accessing services and planning next steps is slowed by generic, overly broad, or poorly structured information. They expressed a strong desire for simple, scenario-based guidance that filters content to their needs — frequently describing the idea of a "flow chart" to visualise and simplify decisions.

## Value and impact

- **Perceived value:** Helps parents get to the right information faster and with less confusion.
- **Civic value:** Reduces misinformation and improves early access to entitlements and support.
- **Measurement:** Time-to-find for key queries; user satisfaction with navigation tools; reduction in misdirected queries.
- **Decision rationale:** This need addresses a common access barrier and was echoed across multiple focus groups with consistent language, for example "flow chart" and "just tell me what applies to us".

## Evidence of success

- Increased speed and confidence in decision-making.
- Reduced demand on professionals for clarification or redirection.
- Improved access to timely support based on actual need.

## Expected outcomes

- Parents engage earlier and more confidently with relevant services.
- Fewer delays due to confusion or irrelevant options.
- Higher trust in the system's ability to support their family's specific journey.
