# Recognise the young person as an individual

## Working formulation

A parent/carer of a young person with SEND needs their child to be recognised as an individual, so decisions and support protect the young person's dignity.

## Wording rationale

The wording preserves the relational and dignity meaning in the evidence. It deliberately avoids reducing the need to a purely functional task because the evidence concerns recognition, individuality and avoiding generic assumptions.

This is marked high sensitivity because small wording changes could remove the dignity claim or unintentionally imply that diagnosis is irrelevant. The intended meaning is that diagnosis should not replace individual understanding.
