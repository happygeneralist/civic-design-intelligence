# Check rights and entitlements

## Working formulation

A parent/carer of a young person with SEND needs to check what rights and entitlements may apply, so they can ask for, secure or challenge support without relying on informal discovery.

## Wording rationale

The wording uses `check` rather than `know` because the evidenced need is not a vague knowledge state. The parent/carer needs a way to establish what they are entitled to ask for and what support routes may be available.

This is separated from pathway-step planning and adjustments because rights and entitlements have a distinct solution vector. They may require legal, statutory, advocacy or entitlement guidance rather than general pathway planning support.

The phrase `without relying on informal discovery` preserves the evidence signal that the parent/carer was researching things herself, did not know what she was entitled to ask for and felt left to work it out alone.

This candidate assumes that parent/carers may need to establish what rights, entitlements or requests are available during pathway planning. This is distinct from comparing options, assessing practical feasibility, coordinating next steps or communicating adjustments.
