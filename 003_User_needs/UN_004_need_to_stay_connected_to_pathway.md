# Stay connected to my pathway even when my mental health or motivation dips, so I don't lose progress or feel overwhelmed starting again

## Summary

Young people with SEND experience fluctuating motivation and mental health that disrupt ongoing pathway planning. When they disengage, the absence of continuity mechanisms, for example saved progress or supportive check-ins, leads to exhaustion and loss of confidence. They express a desire for ways to stay anchored to their plan even when active engagement is not possible.

## Value and impact

- **Perceived value:** Reduces guilt, overwhelm, and the emotional cost of restarting.
- **Civic value:** Supports sustained engagement and improves progression outcomes.
- **Measurement:** Frequency of re-engagement; drop-out recovery rates; user confidence in "getting back on track".
- **Decision rationale:** Anchoring mechanisms can prevent disengagement from becoming permanent exclusion.
