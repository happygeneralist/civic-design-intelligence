# Identify which options match the young person's needs and aspirations, so I can help them decide their next steps

## Summary

Parents report that simply being signposted to resources is not enough, they often feel unequipped to interpret which options are right for their child. Both parents and young people may be unclear on needs, strengths, and available pathways. The ability to confidently match options to aspirations is essential for meaningful planning.

## Value and impact

- **Perceived value:** Helps families move from confusion to clarity when planning next steps.
- **Civic value:** Supports inclusion and equity in decision-making and post-16 transitions.
- **Measurement:** Uptake of planning tools; confidence levels in decision-making; reduced time spent searching.
- **Decision rationale:** Prioritised due to strong emotional tone in user feedback and alignment with pathway improvement goals.

## Evidence of success

- Parents express frustration at being signposted to services where the receiving end doesn't know how to help.
- Desire for a more interpretive or relational layer to guidance.
- Emotional tone: stuck, unsupported, anxious about missing out on opportunities.

## Expected outcomes

- Improved ability to evaluate and compare pathway options.
- Reduced stress for families in planning phase.
- Better alignment between young people's aspirations and chosen options.
