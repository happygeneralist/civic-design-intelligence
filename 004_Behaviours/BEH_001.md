# Discusses pathway options with professionals (e.g. SENCOs, careers advisors, key workers)

### Summary
Parents of young people with SEND engage in discussions with professionals to understand and evaluate post-16 pathways. These conversations are intended to help them interpret their child’s strengths, needs, and realistic options. However, discussions often occur reactively (e.g., during crisis points) or are perceived as unhelpful due to limited professional capacity or unclear next steps.

### Triggers
- School transition points (Year 9 reviews, Year 11 planning)
- Receipt of reports, exam results, or EHCP updates
- Anxiety or uncertainty about next-step options
- Prompt from a professional (e.g. meeting invitation)

### Observed outcomes
- When successful: parents gain clarity, realistic expectations, and actionable next steps  
- When unsuccessful: parents feel dismissed, overwhelmed, or no clearer about their options  
- Variable experience quality depending on local provision and professional expertise

### Barriers (CREATE Action analysis)
- **Cue:** No consistent signal for when to start the conversation  
- **Reaction:** Stress or avoidance due to emotional overload  
- **Evaluation:** Doubts about the usefulness of professional input (“they just signpost me elsewhere”)  
- **Ability:** Parents lack frameworks or confidence to structure the discussion  
- **Timing:** Conversations often occur too late to influence planning  
- **Experience:** Past negative experiences reduce likelihood of future engagement

### Related COM-B factors
- **Capability (Psychological):** Limited interpretive skill in linking aspirations to pathways  
- **Opportunity (Social/Environmental):** Fragmented system and lack of structured planning prompts  
- **Motivation (Reflective & Automatic):** Anxiety, low confidence, and scepticism about available support

### Notes
This behaviour is critical for moving from “information gathering” to “decision-making.” Improving the cues, structure, and perceived value of these discussions could significantly enhance parents’ planning confidence and reduce overwhelm.
