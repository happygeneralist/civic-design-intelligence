# Seeks input from adults who know the young person well

## Working formulation

Parent/carer seeks input from adults who know the young person well so planning reflects day-to-day functioning.

## Priority

Priority: 5 — medium.

This behaviour is prioritised because it supports accurate and dignified planning, but it may sit underneath broader behaviours around correcting assumptions and assessing route viability.

## Triggers and conditions

This behaviour appears likely when the parent/carer believes a planning conversation will be more useful if it includes someone who understands the young person in everyday contexts.

It may be less likely when planning processes only recognise formal roles or do not make space for relational knowledge.

## Interpretation

This candidate assumes that parent/carers may value input from people who know the young person, not only people with formal pathway-planning roles. The behaviour is about bringing contextual knowledge into the decision process.

## Observed or expected outcomes

When supported, this behaviour may improve the fit between pathway decisions and the young person's actual functioning, preferences and support needs.

When unsupported, planning may rely too heavily on unfamiliar adults, generic assumptions or one-off conversations.
