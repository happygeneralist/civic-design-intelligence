# Tries to bring pathway planning forward

## Working formulation

Parent/carer tries to bring pathway planning forward before key education choices become fixed.

## Priority

Priority: 1 — high.

This behaviour is high priority because timing shapes whether later needs can be met. If planning starts too late, realistic options, rights, adjustments, contingency routes and support choices may already be constrained.

## Triggers and conditions

This behaviour appears likely when a parent/carer anticipates that GCSE choices, post-16 applications or other transition decisions are approaching and fears that decisions will arrive all at once.

It may be less likely when there is no clear cue for when planning should begin, when the parent/carer does not know what questions to ask, or when previous support has felt unhelpful.

## Observed or expected outcomes

When supported, this behaviour may help families make decisions with enough time to act, seek clarification, check entitlements and avoid late-stage crisis planning.

When unsupported, parent/carers may still recognise the need to start early but be unable to turn that recognition into effective planning activity.
