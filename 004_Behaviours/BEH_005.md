# Checks whether pathway options are viable in practice

## Working formulation

Parent/carer checks whether pathway options are viable against grades, support needs, travel, anxiety, burnout and day-to-day functioning.

## Priority

Priority: 3 — high.

This behaviour is high priority because it is where nominal options become real or unreal. It connects directly to decision quality, accessibility and whether a pathway can be sustained in practice.

## Triggers and conditions

This behaviour appears likely when a parent/carer is comparing college, training or other post-16 routes and realises that course availability alone is not enough to judge suitability.

It may be triggered by uncertainty about entry requirements, travel, attendance, anxiety, burnout or support arrangements.

## Interpretation

This candidate assumes the parent/carer is testing pathway options against practical and relational constraints. This behaviour connects the abstract option space to the young person's actual life, capacity and support conditions.

## Observed or expected outcomes

When supported, this behaviour may help families avoid routes that are formally available but unsustainable.

When unsupported, the parent/carer may carry complex feasibility analysis alone, or a young person may enter a route that fails because hidden constraints were not considered early enough.
