# Triangulates pathway information across sources

## Working formulation

Parent/carer triangulates pathway information across informal networks, school contacts and their own research to build a fuller picture.

## Priority

Priority: 2 — high.

This behaviour is high priority because it is a visible compensating behaviour. It shows where the system is not providing a sufficiently reliable, joined-up picture and where parent/carers are carrying the work of interpretation themselves.

## Triggers and conditions

This behaviour appears likely when formal guidance feels generic, partial or unable to answer the parent/carer's specific questions.

It may be reinforced when informal sources appear more responsive or practical than official sources, even where those sources may be incomplete or inconsistent.

## Interpretation

The evidence describes reliance on Facebook groups, teacher friends and ad hoc research, alongside a sense that no one can provide the full picture. This suggests a triangulation behaviour: the parent/carer is not simply searching, but reconciling partial and uneven information into something usable for pathway decisions.

## Observed or expected outcomes

This behaviour may help parent/carers discover options, rights or practical considerations that formal guidance does not surface clearly.

It may also increase cognitive load, expose parent/carers to inconsistent advice and make confidence dependent on informal privilege, persistence or network access.
