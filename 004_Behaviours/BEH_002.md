# Compares and shortlists post-16 options to match their young person’s needs and aspirations

### Summary
Parents of young people with SEND review available education, training, and employment options to decide which best suit their child’s strengths, needs, and goals. This often involves evaluating information from colleges, local services, and online sources. Many parents find this process difficult due to fragmented data, unclear eligibility, and uncertainty about what is realistic or achievable.

### Triggers
- Approaching transition milestones (end of Year 9 or 11)
- Receipt of updated EHCP or school report
- Professional recommendation to explore next steps
- Parental motivation to reduce uncertainty about the future

### Observed outcomes
- When successful: parents gain clarity, create a shortlist of realistic options, and feel more confident supporting decision-making  
- When unsuccessful: parents experience confusion, information overload, or decision paralysis  
- Overreliance on anecdotal or incomplete information sources

### Barriers (CREATE analysis)
- **Cue:** No consistent or timely signal to begin comparison activity  
- **Reaction:** Overwhelm and avoidance when faced with too many or unclear options  
- **Evaluation:** Low perceived usefulness of existing information platforms (“they don’t help me match to my child’s needs”)  
- **Ability:** Parents lack structured tools or frameworks to compare options systematically  
- **Timing:** Comparison often occurs too late for meaningful influence on applications or transitions  
- **Experience:** Frustrating and time-consuming processes reduce willingness to repeat

### Related COM-B factors
- **Capability (Psychological):** Limited skills to interpret and compare different pathways  
- **Opportunity (Environmental):** Fragmented or inconsistent information sources; lack of accessible comparison tools  
- **Motivation (Reflective):** Strong intrinsic motivation (desire for clarity) but weakened by anxiety and low confidence

### Notes
This behaviour is central to achieving the user need “Identify which options match the young person’s needs and aspirations.”  

It represents the moment parents translate information into actionable decisions.  
Improving this behaviour likely requires tools that make comparison *easier, more structured, and confidence-building* — for example, curated comparison interfaces or guided shortlisting templates.
