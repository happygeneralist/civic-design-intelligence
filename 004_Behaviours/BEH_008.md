# Returns to pathway decisions after a route has started

## Working formulation

Parent/carer returns to pathway decisions after a route has started to check whether support or choices need to change.

## Priority

Priority: 6 — medium.

This behaviour is prioritised because it connects planning to sustained outcomes. It is slightly lower priority for the initial behaviour set because it occurs after initial decision-making, but it is important for redress and adaptation.

## Triggers and conditions

This behaviour appears likely when a chosen route is not working, when support needs change, or when the young person is struggling to sustain the pathway in practice.

It may be less likely when there is no explicit reflection point, no obvious route back into support, or when parent/carers believe the original decision is fixed.

## Interpretation

This candidate describes a desired reflection stage where families can return, check how things are going and adjust choices or support. This behaviour is currently treated as a candidate behaviour because it may represent a desired future action rather than something already observed.

## Observed or expected outcomes

When supported, this behaviour may help families repair unsuitable plans, change support and avoid continuing with routes that are not working.

When unsupported, families may have to wait for crisis, drop-off or failure before support changes.
