# Chases pathway support

## Working formulation

Parent/carer chases people and recontacts services to keep pathway planning moving when support routes are unclear or unresponsive.

## Triggers and conditions

This behaviour appears when a parent/carer is trying to plan next steps but does not have a clear, reliable route through the support system.

It may be more likely when:

- the parent/carer is passed between people or services
- previous contact turns out to have been with the wrong person or route
- pathway actions depend on other organisations responding
- the parent/carer feels responsible for keeping momentum despite limited capacity

## Interpretation

The behaviour is a compensating behaviour. The parent/carer appears to be doing the work of follow-up, route-finding and escalation because pathway support is not experienced as coordinated or easy to navigate.

This is distinct from comparing options. It is about trying to make the system respond enough for planning to continue.

## Observed or expected outcomes

When the behaviour works, it may unblock actions or eventually connect the family to the right person.

When unsupported, it creates exhaustion, delays, duplicated effort and a sense that pathway planning depends on parent/carer persistence rather than a reliable support process.
