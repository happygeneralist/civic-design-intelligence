# Corrects or challenges inaccurate planning assumptions

## Working formulation

Parent/carer corrects or challenges planning information when it does not reflect the young person's abilities, needs or communication.

## Priority

Priority: 4 — medium-high.

This behaviour is prioritised because inaccurate assumptions can directly distort decisions and support. It may also be emotionally costly, because the parent/carer has to repair misunderstanding while protecting the young person's dignity.

## Triggers and conditions

This behaviour appears likely when a report, conversation or professional judgement does not match the parent/carer's understanding of how the young person functions in daily life.

It may be more likely when the young person can give answers that sound convincing but are inaccurate, especially with unfamiliar adults.

## Interpretation

This candidate assumes that parent/carers may need to intervene when professional understanding is based on partial or misleading interaction. This is a repair behaviour: the parent/carer tries to prevent planning from being built on an inaccurate picture of the young person.

## Observed or expected outcomes

When the behaviour succeeds, planning is more likely to reflect the young person's actual abilities, needs, communication and support conditions.

When unsupported, the burden of correction falls on the parent/carer, and inaccurate assumptions may continue to shape decisions.
