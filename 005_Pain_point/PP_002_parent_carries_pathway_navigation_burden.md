# Parent carries pathway navigation burden

## Working formulation

Parent/carer carries a high pathway navigation burden when support routes are unclear, fragmented or unresponsive.

## Public-source reset note

Retained as a generic candidate civic knowledge object. Source-specific evidence links were removed during the public-source reset. This object should not be treated as reviewed or validated.

## Evidence update note

This object now has traceable public-source evidence from the Essex Ofsted/CQC area SEND inspection report.

The evidence is local inspection evidence. It supports this pain point as a local evidence instance and as a candidate reusable pattern, but it does not prove national prevalence or severity.

## Impact

This pain point can turn pathway planning into sustained unpaid coordination work for the parent/carer. This candidate frames the burden as involving exhaustion, repeated chasing, uncertainty about whether they are speaking to the right people and reduced capacity to plan while also working and caring.

The linked Ofsted/CQC evidence strengthens the communication and support-visibility part of the pain point. It shows that inconsistent communication, unclear support visibility and insufficient communication about plans and support can leave families' questions and anxieties unresolved while they wait for assessments or services.

Severity remains high as a working judgement because the burden may affect the parent/carer's ability to keep planning moving. Confidence remains low because the current public evidence is limited to one local inspection source and this object has not been human reviewed.

## Conditions and causes

The pain point appears to be worsened by:

- unclear entry points for pathway support
- unclear ownership of next-step planning
- repeated signposting or misrouting
- actions that depend on the parent/carer chasing progress
- limited acknowledgement of the load the parent/carer is already carrying
- inconsistent frequency and quality of communication
- insufficient communication about plans, support and available help while families wait
